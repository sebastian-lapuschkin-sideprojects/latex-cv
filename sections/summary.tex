% ===== Current Short Bio (frontier-AI framed) =====
Sebastian Lapuschkin heads the Explainable Artificial Intelligence research group at the Fraunhofer Heinrich Hertz Institute (HHI) in Berlin, directing a team of more than 25 researchers. He received his Ph.D.\ with distinction from the Technische Universität Berlin in 2018 for foundational contributions to Explainable AI (XAI) and interpretable machine learning.

His research investigates the inner workings of modern frontier models: mechanistic interpretability of Transformers and large language models, the attribution of model behavior to training data and internal components, and the use of these insights to debug, control, and compress neural networks. Recent contributions include real-time attribution for LLMs and Vision Transformers (AttnLRP), attention-head-level analysis of in-context learning, and explanation-guided pruning that removes up to 95\% of model parameters without loss of performance.

His foundational methods --- among them Layer-wise Relevance Propagation and Concept Relevance Propagation --- are widely used across academia and industry. He is a committed advocate of open science, releasing open-source toolkits such as Zennit and Quantus to support reproducible AI research, and is a recipient of the Hugo-Geiger-Prize and the 2020 Pattern Recognition Best Paper Award.

Since 2024 he co-organizes the World Conference on eXplainable Artificial Intelligence.
